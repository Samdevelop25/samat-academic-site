AI, Healthcare, and Data Engineering

I work at the intersection of AI, healthcare, and data engineering. I am developing multimodal machine learning models that can continuously monitor human health and predict early risk indicators. My long-term goal is to pursue graduate research at Stanford University, collaborating with Professor Nigam Shah on AI-driven early disease detection and learning health systems.

I specialize in Python, SQL, cloud-scale data pipelines (BigQuery, Snowflake, dbt, Airflow), and building end-to-end ML applications with FastAPI and modern web frameworks.

Current Focus

I am currently designing an AI-powered multimodal personal health assistant that integrates speech, text, wearable, and behavioral signals to detect early signs of mental and cardiometabolic risk.

This work connects directly to biomedical informatics, digital health, and human-centered AI.
