Research Focus

AI-Powered Multimodal Personal Health Assistant

My primary research interest is building an AI-powered multimodal personal health assistant that continuously monitors subtle changes in speech, language, wearable signals, and daily behavior to detect early health risks.

The goal is to identify early indicators of depression, anxiety, chronic stress, sleep disruption, and cardiometabolic risk before they fully manifest clinically.

Key Modalities

  • Speech: prosody, tempo, pauses, vocal stress markers
  • Text: sentiment, semantics, cognitive patterns in journaling or chat
  • Behavior: typing cadence, routine stability, digital interaction patterns
  • Wearables: heart rate, HRV, sleep quality, activity levels

Methodology

  • Multimodal ETL and feature engineering pipelines
  • Self-supervised and supervised models for each modality
  • Fusion architectures combining time-series, text, and audio representations
  • Prototype tools for real-time risk scoring and visualization

Proposal

I am actively developing this project as a potential collaboration with Professor Nigam Shah at Stanford, within the Biomedical Informatics and learning health systems context.
